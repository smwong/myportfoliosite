Sheena Wong: a brief summary

Started here:

I started my graphic design journey doing small projects for friends and family. This included flyers, business cards, menus, invitations, signs, etc.

And then:

In high school and college I learned to use the Adobe line of programs, like Photoshop, Illustrator, and InDesign.

Gaining experience:

Worked a number of entry level stints: digital color corrector, junior designer, QA testing for a mobile app startup, etc.

Currently:

I work full time presently as a graphic designer and image editor for a local photography company. It started out as a small family-run business about 40 years ago, and has since grown into a state-wide operation. Part of my job is to help them find ways to update their branding for the digital age we live in. So far, I've used drag-and-drop programs like MailChimp ad SquareSpace to create HTML templates, but I would love to be able to have more control of overall design by learning web development.

CoderGirl Goals:

I definitely want to get to a point where I can create websites! My experience so far has been mostly print-based projects, so I'm hoping to use the skills I learn in CoderGirl to dive into digital design.



About Me:

But she is an Aquarius, a Photoshop enthusiast, and an Oxford comma stan.
